Our Dataset

We discovered this dataset titled "Border Crossing Entry Data" on the website "Kaggle.com".
Upon further inspection, we found the original dataset on the U.S. Bureau of Transportation's website.
This dataset covers all ports of entry in the U.S. under control of the U.S. government.
Each row represents a mode of transportation encountered (or not encountered) during that month.
Each row contains the port code, port name, state, latitude of the port, longitude of the port.
As well as the mode of transportation and the amount encountered.

https://data.bts.gov/Research-and-Statistics/Border-Crossing-Entry-Data/keg4-3bc2/data

Our Inspiration

Our inspiration was to make an interactive dashboard where one could find summaries of the border crossing statistics.
This reference can be used to explore research questions regarding border crossings in the U.S.
We had a few research questions of our own:
Has the overall traffic at U.S. ports of entry increased or decreased over time?
Are these changes localized to specific ports or regions?
What are the patterns and volumes of the various modes of transport at entry ports into the U.S.?
